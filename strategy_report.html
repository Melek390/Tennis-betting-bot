Back the Favourite After Losing Set 1
Markets over-correct when the favourite drops the opening set — buy them at a discount
+6–8% ROI documented Match Winner market Automatable

Entry Conditions

  • Pre-match favourite (price 1.25–1.60 on Betfair / 40–80¢ on Kalshi)
  • Loses the first set
  • Kalshi price has drifted ≥25% above pre-match price
  • First set was competitive (6-4, 7-5, 7-6 — not 6-0/6-1)
  • Wait: favourite holds first service game of set 2 cleanly before entering

Exit Conditions

  • Profit: Favourite wins set 2 → price snaps back toward pre-match level
  • Stop loss: Favourite goes 0–3 down in set 2 → exit immediately
  • Partial exit: If sets go to 1-1, take 50% profit and hold remainder

Why It Works

Strong favourites win from a set down ~45–55% of the time in best-of-three. Markets systematically overshoot the correction after set 1 because casual bettors abandon the favourite. You're buying a real 45% probability at a 35% market price.

Documented Results

MarketFeeder Pro tested variant: +6.72% ROI (12W/2L) and +8.05% ROI (14W/1L). Confirmed directionally positive across multiple test windows by Betfair Trading Community.

Kalshi: Full compatibility. Set scores are detected via existing tennis WebSocket. Kalshi ATP/WTA match winner markets are already integrated. This slots directly into rules.py — roughly 30 lines of new code.

Fade the Service Break (Back the Broken Server)
Markets overreact to breaks — elite servers recover ~50% of the time from 0–40
Positive EV on filtered matches Match Winner market Very automatable

Entry Conditions

  • Service break occurs (opponent wins the service game)
  • Broken player is the pre-match favourite OR strong server on fast surface
  • Break was "soft" (unforced errors, not aggressive opponent winners)
  • Broken player's price increased ≥20% from pre-break price
  • Do NOT enter on a break in the final game of a set

Exit Conditions

  • Profit: Broken player immediately re-breaks → price snaps back
  • Profit: Broken player holds serve twice → market calms, smaller profit
  • Stop loss: Broken player loses next service game (double break) → exit at 40–50% of stake

Why It Works

Betfair's own research: top ATP servers recover from 0–40 approximately 50% of the time. The market prices a break as near-permanent when statistically it's a coin flip on fast surfaces. Grass/hard court servers are especially undervalued post-break.

Documented Results

Betfair official blog published this as a documented strategy. Unfiltered bot: −1.48% ROI. With server quality + surface filter applied: positive ROI documented by Caan Berry and MarketFeeder Pro independently.

Kalshi: Full compatibility. Break detection = game score drops when server changes → already trackable via WebSocket. Most similar to current Rule 5 architecture — entry on break event, exit on re-break or double-break.

Lay the First Set Winner (Second-Set Slump)
Winning the first set triggers complacency — bet against the first-set winner in set 2
83% win rate in halftime LTD variant Match Winner market Automatable

Entry Conditions

  • First set ends — either player wins it
  • First-set winner's match price is ≤1.56 on Betfair (≤60¢ Kalshi)
  • Best: underdog wins set 1 (larger price overreaction)
  • Preferred surface: clay or WTA (more physical, more second-set slumps)
  • Avoid: 6-0 or 6-1 dominance (no psychological let-down)

Exit Conditions

  • Profit: Other player wins set 2 → price drifts significantly
  • Stop loss: First-set winner goes 4-1 up in set 2 → cut the trade early
  • Target exit: End of set 2 regardless — don't carry into set 3

Why It Works

Ryan Carruthers (professional Betfair tennis trader) built a career around this. Post-set-win complacency is real and documented. The market over-shortens the set winner's price for set 2, creating consistent value on the opponent at 1-0 in sets.

Documented Results

netprofit.tennis ATP data (2016-2019, 8,408 matches): 36% of all ATP matches qualify. Positive EV documented. Betfair Trading Community flagged second-set slump as "one of the most consistent recurring patterns in WTA." Ryan Carruthers trades this full-time.

Kalshi: Full compatibility. Set score data already in MatchState. Kalshi ATP/WTA market is the match winner market — you're buying the trailing player's Yes contract at a discount when they're 0-1 in sets.

Opening Set Price Compression Lay
Lay the set leader at 4-1 or 5-1 risking 3 ticks to gain 50+ ticks on a break back
Asymmetric risk:reward Set Winner or Match Winner Betfair preferred

Entry Conditions

  • Score in any set reaches 4-1, 5-1, 5-2, or 4-2
  • Leading player's match price is within 3-4 ticks of their "set winner" theoretical price (typically 1.12–1.20 Betfair / 83–88¢ Kalshi)
  • Trailing player had at least one break point opportunity recently
  • WTA or ATP clay preferred (more break opportunities)

Exit Conditions

  • Profit: Trailing player breaks back (4-3 or 5-3) → price drifts 50+ ticks
  • Stop loss: Set closes out → accept 2-4 tick loss and move on
  • Caan Berry: "You're risking 3 ticks to make 50+ ticks — reward:risk IS the edge"

Why It Works

The market over-compresses at 4-1/5-1 treating the set as near-certain. Statistically, a break back is still 30-40% likely at this score. The asymmetry (3 ticks risk vs 50+ ticks reward) means you only need a 6-10% win rate to break even — but actual win rate is 25-35%.

Documented Results

Caan Berry's primary recommended strategy for tennis beginners — documented in his Tennis Traders Guide. Daniel Temple's case study on Medium shows a specific 4-1 example: 3-tick risk → 53-tick gain on break back. Win rate lower than 50% but highly profitable long-term due to reward ratio.

Kalshi: Partial compatibility. Kalshi only offers match winner markets (not set winner). At 4-1/5-1 the match price is already at 85-90¢ which is near the ≤60¢ entry cap. Works better on Betfair where you can trade tick-by-tick. For Kalshi, adjust entry cap to 88¢ for this strategy specifically.

Lay the Draw (LTD)
The most documented automated exchange strategy — a goal spikes the draw price, lock profit and exit
70–83% win rate documented Draw market Most popular automated strategy

Entry Conditions

  • Score is 0-0, within first 5-10 minutes
  • Pre-match draw odds between 3.2 and 5.0
  • Home team is pre-match favourite (home win odds 1.7–2.3)
  • Both teams have <10% full-time 0-0 rate in last 8 games
  • Home team scores in 1st half in >50% of matches
  • Away team not set up purely to defend

Exit Conditions

  • Profit: First goal scored → immediately BACK the draw (price spikes 40–80%)
  • Stop loss: 0-0 at 70-72 minutes → close for ~85% of stake loss
  • Alternative: If underdog scores first, lay the underdog — if favourite equalises, exit profitably

Why It Works

Post-goal, casual bettors flood the draw market causing a systematic overreaction in draw price. A goal moves draw odds from ~3.5 to 8-15+ (small lead) or 20-30+ (two-goal lead). The market's overreaction to goals is the documented structural edge.

Documented Results

Betfair Trading Community tested "LTD at halftime" variant: 83% win rate. Sports Trading Life documents a full-time professional trader (David G) who does this exclusively. Win rate 70-80% with proper match selection.

Kalshi: Not available. Kalshi has no active European league draw markets (EPL/La Liga/Serie A season ended). Requires Betfair Exchange. Infrastructure needed: live football score API (API-Football or SportMonks) + Betfair API client. Buildable in ~2-3 weeks.

Back Over 1.5 Goals — Drip Feed System
Enter in stages at 20, 27 and 35 minutes while goalless — a goal locks profit at any point
74% of qualifying matches produce a goal Goals market Very automatable

Entry Conditions (3-drip system)

  • Both teams avg >2.5 goals/game, <10% 0-0 rate
  • Drip 1: Still 0-0 at 20 min → back Over 1.5 Goals (0.35% bankroll)
  • Drip 2: Still 0-0 at 27 min → back Over 1.5 Goals (0.35% bankroll)
  • Drip 3: Still 0-0 at 35 min → back Over 1.5 Goals (0.35% bankroll)

Exit Conditions

  • Profit: Any goal scored → immediately LAY Over 1.5 for entire matched stake → locked green
  • Half-time review: If still 0-0, evaluate based on match stats before deciding to hold or exit at ~25% loss
  • Stop: If 0-0 at 75 min → close position

Why It Works

Markets mechanically inflate the no-goal probability while ignoring that teams are still pushing in high-scoring matches. You drip in at progressively better prices as time passes goalless, improving average entry. One goal anywhere in the remaining 55+ minutes locks profit on all positions.

Documented Results

Betfair Trading Community backtested second-half variant (back at 60 min if 0-0): 74.3% qualifying matches produce a goal at odds typically 1.40–1.50 against a 1.35 break-even. Profitable at scale with proper match selection.

Kalshi: Not available. Kalshi has no Over/Under goals markets. Requires Betfair Exchange. Same infrastructure requirement as Strategy 1 — once Betfair client is built, adding this strategy is a small increment.

Simplest football strategy — back a goal in the second half when 0-0 at the 60th minute

Entry Conditions

  • Both teams have <10% 0-0 rate in last 8 games
  • Both teams score in >50% of second halves
  • 4-5+ shots on target between 45-60 min (match is still open)
  • Back Over 0.5 Goals Second Half at current in-play price

Exit Conditions

  • Profit: Any goal scored → LAY immediately, price drops to 1.01-1.03
  • Stop loss: If 0-0 at 75 min → exit (~30-40% stake loss)

Teams that are 0-0 at 60 minutes get increasingly aggressive for the remaining 30 minutes. The market hasn't fully adjusted to the concentrated remaining-time probability. Back the First Goalscorer's Team
Back the team that scores first — win probability jumps +25% on average, markets lag 60-90 seconds
80%+ win rate after away team scores first Match Winner market Automatable with goal feed

Entry Conditions

  • First goal scored between minutes 15 and 75
  • Enter within 90 seconds of goal notification
  • Best case: Away team scores first (larger odds drift = more opportunity)
  • Avoid: goals in first 15 min (odds haven't fully moved yet) or after 75 min (too little time)
  • Back the scoring team's match winner market at in-play price

Exit Conditions

  • Profit: Second goal by the same team → lock and exit immediately
  • Stop: Equaliser scored → exit with pre-agreed loss budget (~40-60% of stake)
  • Hold: If leads 1-0 into 80+ minutes, hold to settlement for maximum profit

Why It Works

Smarkets published data: scoring the first goal increases win probability by +25 percentage points on average. Markets take 60-90 seconds to fully re-price after a goal. Entering in this window captures the lag. Away teams have the most pronounced effect since their pre-match underdog odds create the largest absolute move.

Documented Results

Smarkets published: home team scores first → wins 83.3%. Away team scores first → wins ~80%. First goal in mins 1-15: scoring team wins 64%. First goal in mins 76-90: scoring team wins 87.5%. Smarkets flagged away-first-goal as "very profitable" in their official strategy guide.

Kalshi: Partial. Kalshi's J-League (30 markets, KXJLEAGUEGAME) has match winner markets that could work for this strategy right now. European leagues need Betfair. J-League is lower liquidity but a viable test bed before European seasons restart in August.
